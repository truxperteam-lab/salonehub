⚖️ Sierra Leonean Laws & Website Compliance
Key laws every Sierra Leonean & website owner should know
Data Privacy

🔒 Sierra Leone Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA)

  • Who it applies to: Any person/business processing personal data of Sierra Leoneans
  • User rights: Access, correction, deletion, portability, withdraw consent
  • Website duties: Must have Privacy Policy, get consent before data collection, report breaches within 72hrs
  • Regulator: NDPC — ndpc.gov.ng
  • Penalty: Up to Le10M or 2% of annual gross revenue
Cybercrime

🌐 Cybercrimes Act 2015 (Amended 2024)

  • Hosting false/misleading information online is an offence
  • Identity theft: up to 7 years imprisonment
  • Hacking: up to 3 years imprisonment
  • Cyberstalking and online harassment are criminal offences
  • Regulator: Sierra Leone Police Cybercrime Unit, ACC
Website Requirements

📋 What Every Sierra Leonean Website Must Have

  • ✅ Privacy Policy — explains data collection & use
  • ✅ Terms & Conditions — rules for using your site
  • ✅ Disclaimer — especially for financial or legal information
  • ✅ Contact details — physical address or email
  • ✅ If collecting payments: BSL-licensed gateway required (Paystack, Flutterwave)
Labour

👷 Labour Act — Key Points

  • Minimum wage: Le70,000/month (2024)
  • Work hours: Max 8hrs/day, 40hrs/week
  • Overtime: 1.5x normal pay
  • Maternity leave: 12 weeks with full pay
  • Annual leave: Min 6–12 working days
Tax

🧾 Tax Laws — Key Points

  • VAT: 7.5% on goods & services
  • Company tax: 30% (large), 20% (medium), 0% (small businesses under Le25M revenue)
  • PAYE: 7%–24% based on income bracket
  • Filing deadline: June 30 annually
  • Authority: NRA SL — firs.gov.ng
